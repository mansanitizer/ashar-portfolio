Bringing the Radio Back

Spotify killed the radio starâ€”or at least, the radio DJ. While algorithmic playlists are convenient, they lack soul. They lack the context, the trivia, and the human connection that a great radio host provides.

Pirate DJ was an experiment to bring that back using AI.

The Experience

You connect your Spotify account. The system analyzes your "On Repeat" or "Discover Weekly" playlist. Then, between every 3-4 songs, it injects a short audio segment.

But it's not a generic "Up next is..." voice. The AI DJ:

  • Reads the Vibe: Analyzes the tempo and energy of the upcoming tracks.
  • Drops Knowledge: Pulls trivia about the artist or the album from the web.
  • Has Personality: You can choose your host. Want a smooth jazz voice or a high-energy pop host?

Under the Hood

This project was a mashup of the Spotify Web Playback SDK and ElevenLabs API.

  1. Analysis: We fetch the playlist metadata.
  2. Scripting: GPT-4 writes a short script connecting the previous song to the next one (e.g., "That was 'Dreams' by Fleetwood Mac. Speaking of dreams, this next track...").
  3. Synthesis: The script is sent to ElevenLabs for ultra-low latency TTS generation.
  4. Playback: The audio buffer is injected into the audio stream via the Web Audio API.

Lessons

The main challenge was timing. Radio is seamless. An AI pausing for 5 seconds to "think" ruins the flow. We solved this by pre-generating the "links" (the DJ talk) while the previous song was playing.
